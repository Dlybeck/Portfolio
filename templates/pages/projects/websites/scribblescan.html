ScribbleScan

Check it out!

ScribbleScan is a fully online OCR service I solo-developed as my capstone project and have since spun off into ScribbleScan LLC. It is a web application that utilizes AI to provide highly accurate handwriting digitization on just about any handwritten text, even preserving images or non-textual content in a document. It outperforms leading OCR and AI-based OCR competitors by an average of ~11-20% (depending on the service) in character accuracy on particularly complicated handwriting. Initial Proposal (January 2025)

This is the initial proposal I submitted for my capstone project. This document shows my initial goals and plans for the project. Looking back, it's interesting to see what I stuck with (things like the UI export options...) and what I ended up changing in the process (target audience, some features...)

February 7th 2025

This was the first working version of ScribbleScan. As you can tell, the digitization results looked completely different, splitting each line into a section and showing the result line-by-line. The results are also noticeably worse than today. But despite the rough look, you can tell this matched the original design I had in mind in the proposal. I would say this is the first working "proof of concept" and I was very happy with it at the time.

February 8th 2025

Despite only being a day later, I decided to include this milestone since it highlights some large improvements to the UI. Here you can see the size of the "tiles" is much larger, and the digitize button is hidden after it has been clicked. This change allows a much easier viewing experience, and can still be seen on scribblescan.app today.

March 12th 2025

One month after the "proof of concept," a lot of work had gone into adding smaller important features. This included exporting any results as a PDF or copying them to clipboard—both for all pages digitized or just 1 page. Along with that, support for corrections and accuracy calculations were added. Here the focus shifted from being a proof-of-concept to more of a usable tool. I wanted to ensure users would be able to use the tool, not just see what it did.

April 28th 2025

This is the version I submitted and presented in front of everyone for my capstone project. Compared to the previous snapshot, here you can see even more tweaks and additions, including a batch page limit, Google sign-in and a toned down UI. While this is not a full product, I would consider this a fully finished tool. It does everything it NEEDS to do, takes input and properly outputs. While there are no extras, it is definitely complete.

June 25th 2025

While this snapshot is nearly two months after the previous one, it has by far the most improvements. All of the work added from here on out is after I graduated—this is me starting ScribbleScan LLC and working on it full time. Most of the additions aren't noticeable from this screenshot, but can all be seen at scribblescan.app today. In this version there are full profiles and support, a full cloud-hosted database, paid tiers and respective app functionalities and many more unlisted features. I would call this a complete product.

July 1st 2025

Finally, about 1 week after the last snapshot, I would say scribblescan.app is finished :)

The biggest and most important change visible here is the addition of a demo mode! Since anonymous users can be expensive and can't ACTUALLY test the service, I added a demo mode that allows users to "digitize" (pre-calculated) select pages of tough handwriting examples. This allows users to experience the service in action and see first-hand what this service is capable of, without posing a financial burden on the service.
